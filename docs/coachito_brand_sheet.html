The thinking

"Where racademy whispered private members' club, Coachito says your trusted coach, in your pocket."

Why the diminutive. Padel was born in Acapulco and grew up in Madrid and Buenos Aires — it is, before anything else, a Spanish-language sport. The "-ito" suffix is intimate, affectionate, daily: un cafecito, un momentito, mi coachito. The name carries the sport's cultural DNA without trying.

Why the ball is the only bright thing. The whole brand is restrained — clay, cream, ink, stone. One element pops: the yellow ball that is the dot of the "i." Not a decoration above it, the dot itself. It's the brand's sense of humor, used exactly once.

Clay is the new primary (replaces racademy's forest) — warmer, sunnier, references the padel court itself. Ball is the wildcard: a true padel-yellow that appears only in the logo dot. Never as a UI accent, never as a button. Its scarcity is what makes the wordmark instantly recognizable. Ink replaces forest as the dark surface — same dramatic weight, but with a warmer undertone that sits naturally next to clay.

Six rules
  • The ball is sacred. That yellow ball IS the dot of the "i" — never just a decoration floating above it. It appears only in the logo. Never as a UI accent, never as a button, never as a chart color. Its scarcity is what makes the wordmark recognizable at a glance.
  • Italic is for warmth, not emphasis. Use italic Fraunces when the brand is speaking personally — greetings, achievements, taglines, Spanish flourishes. Don't italicize for stress (use a hairline divider or a tracked-caps label instead).
  • Spanish is a seasoning, not a requirement. Sprinkle ¡Bien hecho!, poco a poco, tu coach into branded moments — never into UI strings. Save still says "Save."
  • Clay is the brand. Ink is the night. Clay for hero surfaces, CTAs, branded moments. Ink for dark mode and PDF body type. Cream and bone for backgrounds. Stone for muted text. That's the whole palette.
  • No shadows. Hairlines only. Carried from the original design system. Borders are 0.5px solid var(--hairline). No drop shadows, no glows, no gradients, no Material.
  • Sentence case, always. Including in tracked-caps labels — those are stylistic, not semantic. The string itself is sentence case.
